Mapping Adereon: A Fantasy Cartography Project

By Ian Spangler

LOCALES ACROSS North America are experiencing what seems to be a revolution in craft beer. To date, there are over 7,000 scattered across the continent, including Hawaii.

EVEN COMPANIES who don't make beer have been caught up in this transnational brew-craze. Aztec Aquaponics, a small company based out of Albuquerque, specializes in using beer brewing byproducts for their aquaponic operations. Their produce, sustainable fish and lettuce greens, supply an artisan fast-casual national restaurant chain.

THE ABOVE MAP was commissioned for Aztec in order to assist with their current site-selection for a new location. In addition to locating a high-density area of breweries to maximize their aquaponic production, their supply chain requires that they cannot transport the brewery byproducts more than 25 km.

WHERE should they locate? Hex binning brewery locations in North America and Hawaii offers a potential answer. Hex binning is a technique of spatial analysis that involves creating an array of equally-sized hexagons that measure the density of a phenomena. While I hope that this analysis benefits Aztec, it might also be of use to discerning beer drinkers and craft enthusiasists across the continent as they decide where they might settle down.

ALL TOLD – and setting aside the bursting of any craft beer bubbles, so to speak – Aztec is in a good position to select a new location. Coastal regions appear the most promising when it comes to craft beer proximity. The greater Seattle, San Francisco, and San Diego regions would be successful on the Pacific Coast, while Boston, Washington DC, and Jersey City all share a high alcohol content on the eastern seaboard. Looking in between, Denver, Chicago, and St. Louis also prove likely candidates. However, more granular analysis is necessary to determine anything past these preliminary suggestions.
